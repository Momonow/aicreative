# Routing And Briefs

## Minimum Brief

Capture only what changes production:

- viewer and desired action
- destination platform
- duration and aspect ratio
- supplied footage/assets
- reference creative or style
- whether speech, captions, music, and legal text are required
- deadline or cost constraint
- approval points the user wants

Do not delay a specific edit with broad discovery. For an open-ended project, define the audience, platform, priority, and variation strategy before choosing a visual format.

## Route By Source

- Existing footage only: `video-post-production`, then `talking-head-recut` when appropriate.
- Generated people or scenes: `ai-video-generation` plus `video-post-production`.
- Motion design: `hyperframes` and its routed specialist.
- Hybrid presenter plus proof: `ai-video-generation`, then `pip-composite` or `stacked-format`, then `video-post-production`.
- Paid-social ad: add `admachin-video-ads`.

## Route By Risk

- Identity-sensitive: approve anchor and run multi-point continuity QA.
- Regulated copy: lock exact wording before generation and use in-house captions.
- Interface footage: prioritize legibility and hide private selections/data.
- Costly generation: pilot one, generate sequentially, persist task IDs, preserve accepted units.
- Multi-person scene: test voice attribution as well as lip-sync; one speaker per clip is safer.
